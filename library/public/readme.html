Time tracker for MapTool


This Read Me can be re-read in MapTool at any time:
  1. Go to File -> 'Add On Libraries'.
  2. Select Time_tracker from the list of add-ons.
  3. Click the Read Me button.


What is this?

DMs deserve nice things. Time_tracker is designed to help you keep track of a bunch of in-game stuff to make your life easier. Your players are having enough fun. It's ok for DMs to have nice things. A few things to note...

  • The time tracker is (at least for now) specific to the Forgotten Realms setting for Dungeons and Dragons. It tracks dates according to the Calendar of Harptos and illustrates the phases of Sel没ne.
  • Use this resource to help ease the DM info-tracking workload, and perhaps to bolster a description when creative energy is in a lull. Please don't consider this resource as something intended to limit your DMing in any way. For example, the time tracker plots moon cycles, but if you story calls for a dark night with a new moon then just fudge and/or hand-wave it as needed to tell your story.
  • I've implemented a clock with hours and minutes, but this isn't the typical way residents of Faer没n would tell the time. The clock is more for the behind-the-scenes use of the DM, and for the convenience of measuring the passing of time in hours such as for short and long rests.
  • The time tracker represents sun and moon rise/set cycles and moon phases, assuming are the viewer is looking towards the south from the northern hemisphere (e.g. on the Sword Coast). The viewer has a very wide angle >180-degree lens, so the left side of the screen roughly represents 'east' and the right side of the screen roughly represents 'west', but all of this is only a visual representation/approximation, not a 3D simulation.


Using the time tracker

When you first added the Time_tracker Add-on Library, a token was created (the words "Time_tracker" over a crescent moon). If you haven't already done so, select that token and go to Window -> Selected. You'll see some macro buttons in the 'Selected' dock (this is on the right side of the screen in my set-up, but might be elsewhere for you). Go to Window -> Campaign. You'll see a 'Campaign' dock, which is where campaign-wide macros can go. You should be able to drag and drop the macros from the Selected pane to the Campaign pane.

Once you've dragged and dropped your macros into the Campaign dock, right click on them, select 'Edit', go to the 'Details' tab, and change the 'Hot Key' setting to something suitable to allow quick and easy access throughout your gaming.

Once the macros are in the Campaign dock, there is no further need for the Time_tracker_macros token. You can delete that token.

If you need to run the setup process again, resetting the Time_tracker to its default settings and recreating the Time_tracker_macros token, you can do so by copy/pasting the following into the chat window: 

[macro("onFirstInit@lib:icarean.0001.Time_tracker"):""]

If you need to manually alter the settings (e.g. to jump the tracker to a distant year), you can do so by copy/pasting the following into the chat window: (replacing YOURYEAR, YOURDAY, and YOURTIME with your preferred numbers) 

  • [setLibProperty("currentYear", YOURYEAR, "lib:icarean.0001.Time_tracker"):""]
  • [setLibProperty("currentDay", YOURDAY, "lib:icarean.0001.Time_tracker"):""]
  • [setLibProperty("currentTime", YOURTIME, "lib:icarean.0001.Time_tracker"):""]

Known bugs/issues

  • Increasing the month just adds 30 days, so the day-of-month changes in a counter-intuitive way when there are extra days (festivals) between the months. I'm not certain whether longer term I'll leave things this way or change it to try to maintain day-of-month.
  • Updating the current day in the time tracker view doesn't update the highlighted day in the calendar view.
  • Areas of the time tracker are selectable in ways that undermines UI joy (e.g. double-clicking on the hills image highlights that image).
